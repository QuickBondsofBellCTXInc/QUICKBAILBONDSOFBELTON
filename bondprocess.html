2020 Fraud Prevention Act (FPA) — Federal Automated Verification & Enforcement (FAVE) Act

Overview:
The 2020 Fraud Prevention Act (FPA), enacted as the Federal Automated Verification & Enforcement (FAVE) Act, mandates that all government agencies eliminate direct cash, card, or bank payments from the public. All transactions must be processed digitally through licensed intermediaries, with strict personal identity verification.

Key Provisions:

  • Digital-Only Payments: Cash, credit/debit cards, and direct bank transfers are prohibited. Payments must flow through FAVE-approved digital platforms.
  • Identity Verification: Every transaction is tied to a verified personal identity using government-issued ID, biometric authentication, or approved digital verification methods.
  • Authorized Digital Payment Methods:
    • Government-Contracted Cash Terminal Deposits (Voucher Receipts) – secure kiosks issuing transaction vouchers
    • Mobile Banking Platforms (ID Verified) – apps requiring identity authentication
    • In-Store Retail-Clerk Cash Code Deposits – approved retail partners accepting cash linked to a digital ID code
  • Licensed Intermediaries: Only federally certified private entities may process payments, acting as secure financial buffers.
  • Transaction Logging: All payments are recorded in the Public Transaction Network (PTN), including payer ID, intermediary credentials, and government recipient.

Impact:

  • Reduces fraud and identity theft
  • Centralizes and secures public financial transactions
  • Creates a regulated market for licensed intermediaries, especially in courts and bail systems
